MyFootPath Overview

MyFootPath was my first exposure to the world of development. I was originally an Intern and grew into a web developer. I quickly learned that my job could be automated by writing a few simple scripts. THis grew and before I knew it I was helped develop a new tool called the new-form-builder.

My Footpath has been listed in the top 50 in the inc 500. This means they were in the top 50 of the largest growing companies in the US. To put this into perspective by the time I left they had grown over 3000 percent from the time of my stay to the end of my stay.

As the company grew so did deman for my tool and more functionality had to built into it. I helped train teams of people to use this tool that was responsible for every single dollar of revenue for this small company

Shown on the right is the new form builder I helped to build and maintain. The first image is a listing of all forms and which ones still needed to be converted over to the new system. This also gave us access to the editor when the user selects a form for a specific school. Inside of the editor (image 2) we can see all of the fields that could be edited and what properties one could give to each field. In the third image we have the specific settings and custom validation that needed to be included onto the page. Take for instance clients would have us reject a client if he or she was under 18 and lived in California for specific schools. These types of scenarios were numerous and had to be included in the application's use case. The last image is a fully converted form where we can see a visual difference.

Much of the work was done on the back-end since each hard-coded form had to be converted to its respective database format. For example a field’s name, validation, and values had to be converted from hard html to MySQL rows and fields. Many Scripts had to be written since our older forms were dynamic in structure and we had more than 1000 different forms to convert. During my stay at MyFootPath I converted over 500 forms and helped to maintain them.
